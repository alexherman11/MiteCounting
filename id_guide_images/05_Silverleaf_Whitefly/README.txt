Silverleaf Whitefly (Bemisia tabaci)
=====================================
Category: Pest

Images:
  whitefly_adults_shells_pupa.jpeg - Whitefly adults, empty shells, and pupa
  whitefly_immature_life_stages.png - Life stages: (A) Newly laid eggs, (B) Older amber eggs,
    (C) 1st instar nymph, (D) 2nd instar nymph, (E) 3rd instar nymph,
    (F) 4th instar nymph, (G) Late 4th instar "pupa"/"red-eyed nymph", (H) Exuviae after adult emergence

ID Notes:
  - Larvae and pupa lack antennae and legs and are flat against the bottom of the leaf
  - Pupa have visible red eyes
  - Pupa and larvae have some visible yellow parts of the body
  - Empty shells have a t-shaped slit and are white/clear (adult has already emerged)
  - Eggs are tiny and very oval shaped, may be laid in a circle
  - Adults are white, with wings, antennae, legs, and red eyes
